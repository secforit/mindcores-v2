Neuro-linguistic programming (NLP)

NLP is used as a method of personal development through promoting skills, such as self-reflection, confidence, and communication. The technique to change thoughts and behaviors to help achieve desired outcomes for them. Is is also applied commercially to achieve work-orientated goals, such as improved productivity or job progression.

NLP uses perceptual, behavioral, and communication techniques in order to  make it easier in changing thoughts and actions. NLP detects and modify unconscious biases or limitations of an individual’s map of the world. Is not hypnotherapy but it operates through the conscious use of language to bring about changes in thoughts and behavior. The framework involves rapport-building, information-gathering, and goal-setting.

NLP relies on language processing but should not be confused with natural language processing, which shares the same abbreviation. It identifys the patterns of thoughts and behaviors of successful individuals and to teach them to others.

Its uses include treatment of

* phobias and generalized anxiety disorders or GAD

* improvement of workplace performance

* personal happiness and overall wellbeing

* health-related conditions

* weight management

* substance misuse

* social problems

* improved psychological symptoms and life quality

Techniques

- **Anchoring** : Turning sensory experiences into triggers for certain emotional states.

- **Rapport** : The practitioner tunes into the person by matching their physical behaviors to improve communication and response through empathy.

- **Swish pattern** : Changing patterns of behavior or thought to come to a desired instead of an undesired outcome.

- **Visual/kinesthetic dissociation (VKD)** : Trying to remove negative thoughts and feelings associated with a past event.

The effectiveness of NLP is challenging for several reasons because untill yet

has not been subject to the same standard of scientific rigor as more established therapies, such as cognitive behavioral therapy or CBT.

An attitude of NLP is that our body, our emotional life and our thought system influence each other. For example, by changing your posture, you can change emotional states and thoughts. This also works the other way around: When we change our thoughts, our posture automatically changes.

VAKOG – our five senses in NLP

The abbreviation of the five sensory channels are refering to:

V for visual: seeing

A for auditory: listening to sounds, tones

K is for kinesthetic: feeling through touch, touching our skin, but also stomach pain and tension

O for olfactory: smelling

G for gustatory: tasting
