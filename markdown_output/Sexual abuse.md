Sexual abuse

Sexual abuse is a serious violation of an individual's rights, involving unwanted and harmful sexual behavior. This can include a wide range of acts, from verbal harassment to physical assault. It often leads to profound psychological and emotional trauma for the victims, affecting their mental health, relationships, and overall well-being. Prevention and awareness are crucial in addressing this issue, as well as providing support and resources for survivors.

Sexual abuse in couples refers to a range of abusive behaviors that occur within intimate relationships, where one partner exerts power and control over the other through sexual coercion, manipulation, or violence. It can manifest in various forms, including unwanted sexual advances, forced sexual acts, or the use of intimidation to enforce compliance.

Understanding the dynamics of sexual abuse in couples is important, as it often involves complex emotional ties, making it difficult for victims to recognize or leave the abusive situation. Many victims may feel isolated, ashamed, or afraid to seek help, which can perpetuate the cycle of abuse.

Addressing sexual abuse in couples requires awareness, support, and intervention to help those affected escape the abusive environment and to promote healthier, consensual relationships.
