**Psychodynamic Psychotherapy**

Psychodynamic psychotherapy is a recognised scientifical based psychoterapy methods and is based on the fundamentals of psychoanalysis. It aims to explore and understand the interaction and the interplay between the conscious and unconscious minds. That is, some of the symptoms and causes of people's discomfort are not on the surface, but on a deeper level.

**The most important basics**

Psychodynamic therapy consists of a number of theories, the most famous of which are Freud, Erikson and Carl Jung. Some of their central premises are the following:

Existence of the unconscious or the subconscious

Psychological conflicts

The experiences of childhood have a great influence on our whole lives

Another key to psychodynamic therapy has to do with the relationship between psychotherapist and client

The symptoms are compromise solutions

**The highlights of psychodynamic theories**

Psychology of the ego, Object Relations Theory, Attachment theory, Psychology of the Self, Interpersonal psychology, Relationship psychoanalysis

**When psychodynamic therapy can be helpful**

Psychodynamic therapy focuses on working on different topics

It can promote self-knowledge, starting with understanding how we really are and how we really behave, to understand the origin of many of the own limitations. It enables us to illuminate the present and at the same time come to terms with the past.

It can also help us build high-quality bonds, relationships, understand and recognize our feelings.

The most important thing is that you feel comfortable. Apart from the different approaches and working tools, the most important thing at the beginning of a therapy is that the patient feels comfortable in his or her therapeutic space.

To achieve this, it's a good idea to ask the therapist how they work and pay attention to how it makes you feel. Sometimes it is necessary to ask for a referral or look for another therapist.

**Positive and Transcultural Psychotherapy (PPT)**

Positive and Transcultural Psychotherapy is a psychotherapeutic method of **humanistic psychodynamic** psychotherapy and is based on a positive conception of **human nature.** It is an integrative method that includes humanistic, systemic, psychodynamic and cognitive-behavioral elements. It should not be confused with positive psychology.

**The main goal** of PPT is to help, identify and cultivate the abilities strengths, resources, and potentials. This approach combines elements from different modalities of psychotherapy, including:

* a humanistic perspective on human nature and the therapeutic alliance

* a psychodynamic understanding of mental and psychosomatic disorders

* a systemic approach that takes into account family, culture, work and the environment, as well as a practical self-help

* a goal-oriented five-step therapy process that integrates techniques from different therapeutic methods

**The PPT is characterized** by its conflict-centered and resource-oriented approach, which is influenced by transcultural observations from more than twenty different cultures. Located between manualized cognitive behavioral therapy and process-oriented analytical psychotherapy, PPT uses a semi-structured approach to diagnosis, treatment, post-therapeutic self-help, and training.

**Key features** of the PPT method:

Integrative psychotherapy method

Humanistic psychodynamic

Method Cohesive, integrated therapeutic system

Conflict-centered short-term method

Culturally sensitive method

Use of stories, anecdotes and wisdom

Innovative interventions and techniques

Application in psychotherapy, in other medical disciplines, in counselling, in education, in prevention, in management and in further education

The three **main principles** or pillars of the PPT are:

* The Principle of Hope

* The principle of balance

* The principle of counselling

**It is defined trough:**

*** Transcultural approach**

*** Balance model**

**Biological-physical** , rational-intellectual, socio-emotional, and imaginative-value-oriented aspects of daily life

**Physical activities** and perceptions, such as eating, drinking, tenderness, sexuality, sleep, relaxation, sports, appearance and clothing

**Professional achievements and skills** , such as crafts, housekeeping, gardening, education and training, and money management;

relationships and forms of contact with partners, family, friends, acquaintances and strangers; social engagement and activities;

**Future plans, religious/spiritual practices** , purpose/meaning, meditation, reflection, death, beliefs, ideas and development of visions or imagination.

*** Model Dimensions**

*** Conflict Model**

*** Narrative Approach with Stories and Wisdom**

*** Five-Step Concept**

All people have the potential for each of these areas, but some may be more pronounced or neglected due to differences in upbringing and environment. The vital energies, activities and reactions are influenced by and associated with these four areas

**PPT is used to treat** various mental health conditions, including mood disorders, neurotic disorders, stress-related disorders, somatoform disorders, personality disorders, and behavioral syndromes acording to ICD.

PPT is succesful into traditional individual therapy, couples, families and groups. It is also used in psychiatry, also particularly effective in (post-)psychotic disorders and in group settings
